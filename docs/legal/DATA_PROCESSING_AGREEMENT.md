## 1. DEFINITIONS

For the purposes of this DPA:

**"UK GDPR"** means the UK General Data Protection Regulation as it forms part of the law of England and Wales, Scotland and Northern Ireland by virtue of section 3 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018, as amended from time to time.

**"DPA 2018"** means the Data Protection Act 2018.

**"Data Protection Legislation"** means the UK GDPR and DPA 2018, together with any subordinate legislation and regulatory guidance issued by the Information Commissioner's Office ("ICO") from time to time.

**"Personal Data"**, **"Special Category Data"**, **"Processing"**, **"Data Subject"**, **"Data Breach"**, and **"Supervisory Authority"** have the meanings given to them in the UK GDPR.

**"Controller Personal Data"** means the personal data of the Controller's clients and prospective clients that is processed by One Assess on behalf of the Controller via the Service.

**"Service"** means the One Assess fitness assessment software platform as described in the Main Agreement.

**"Sub-Processor"** means any third party engaged by One Assess to process Controller Personal Data.

## 2. SCOPE AND PURPOSE

2.1 This DPA applies to all processing of Controller Personal Data carried out by One Assess in connection with the provision of the Service.

2.2 One Assess processes Controller Personal Data solely in its capacity as a data processor acting on the instructions of the Controller.

2.3 The Controller acknowledges that it is the data controller in respect of its clients' personal data and is responsible for:
- Establishing and maintaining a valid lawful basis for processing under UK GDPR Article 6;
- Obtaining explicit consent from data subjects for the processing of Special Category Data under UK GDPR Article 9, where consent is relied upon as the lawful basis;
- Providing data subjects with a fair processing notice that accurately describes the processing carried out via the Service;
- Ensuring that any assessment of clients under 18 years of age is carried out only with verifiable parental or guardian consent.

## 4. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

In accordance with UK GDPR Article 28(3), One Assess shall:

**4.1 Process only on instructions.** Process Controller Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, as set out in this DPA and the Main Agreement, unless required to do so by applicable law, in which case One Assess shall inform the Controller of that legal requirement before processing unless prohibited from doing so.

**4.2 Confidentiality.** Ensure that persons authorised to process the Controller Personal Data are subject to appropriate obligations of confidentiality, whether by contract, professional duty, or statutory obligation.

**4.3 Security.** Implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect Controller Personal Data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. Such measures shall include as a minimum those described in Schedule 2 to this DPA.

**4.4 Sub-processing.** Not engage Sub-Processors except as set out in Schedule 1, or as subsequently notified to the Controller, subject to the requirements of Clause 6.

**4.5 Data subject rights.** Taking into account the nature of the processing, provide the Controller with reasonable assistance in responding to requests from data subjects exercising their rights under the Data Protection Legislation, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

**4.6 Controller compliance assistance.** Provide the Controller with reasonable assistance in ensuring compliance with obligations under UK GDPR Articles 32–36, including security obligations, breach notification obligations, data protection impact assessments, and prior consultation with the ICO.

**4.7 Deletion or return.** Upon termination of the Main Agreement, and at the choice of the Controller, delete or return all Controller Personal Data and delete existing copies, unless applicable law requires storage of the personal data. One Assess will confirm completion of deletion within 30 days of termination.

**4.8 Audit and information.** Make available to the Controller all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and, upon reasonable written notice of no less than 30 days, permit and contribute to audits and inspections conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, subject to confidentiality obligations.

**4.9 Notification of unlawful instructions.** Inform the Controller promptly if, in One Assess's opinion, an instruction given by the Controller infringes the Data Protection Legislation.

## 5. CONTROLLER OBLIGATIONS

5.1 The Controller warrants that it has and will maintain throughout the term of this DPA a valid lawful basis for the processing of Controller Personal Data under the Data Protection Legislation.

5.2 The Controller warrants that, where Special Category Data is processed (including posture photographs and health screening data), it has obtained explicit, informed consent from each data subject prior to that data being entered into the Service.

5.3 The Controller shall ensure that its use of the Service complies with all applicable laws, including the Data Protection Legislation, and shall not instruct One Assess to process Controller Personal Data in a manner that would constitute a breach of the Data Protection Legislation.

5.4 The Controller is responsible for ensuring that any client report or assessment output generated by the Service is reviewed by a qualified fitness professional before being acted upon, and is used for fitness coaching purposes only.

## 6. SUB-PROCESSORS

6.1 The Controller grants One Assess general written authorisation to engage the Sub-Processors listed in Schedule 1.

6.2 One Assess shall impose on each Sub-Processor data protection obligations equivalent to those set out in this DPA.

6.3 One Assess shall notify the Controller of any intended changes to Sub-Processors by updating Schedule 1 and notifying the Controller via the email address on the Controller's account, with a minimum of 30 days' notice before the change takes effect. If the Controller objects to the change on reasonable grounds relating to data protection, the Parties shall discuss the objection in good faith. If no resolution is reached within 14 days, the Controller may terminate the Main Agreement on written notice.

6.4 One Assess remains fully liable to the Controller for the performance of any Sub-Processor's obligations under this DPA.

## 7. INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

7.1 One Assess shall not transfer Controller Personal Data outside the United Kingdom except where appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR Chapter V.

7.2 Where a Sub-Processor is located outside the UK, One Assess shall ensure that transfers are covered by an appropriate transfer mechanism, which may include UK adequacy regulations, the International Data Transfer Agreement ("IDTA"), or the International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses ("UK Addendum").

7.3 The relevant transfer mechanisms applicable to current Sub-Processors are noted in Schedule 1.

## 8. SECURITY AND DATA BREACHES

8.1 One Assess shall implement and maintain the technical and organisational security measures described in Schedule 2.

8.2 One Assess shall notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within 48 hours of becoming aware, of any confirmed or reasonably suspected Data Breach affecting Controller Personal Data.

8.3 Breach notifications shall include, to the extent reasonably available at the time: a description of the nature of the breach; the categories and approximate number of data subjects and records concerned; the likely consequences of the breach; and the measures taken or proposed to address the breach.

8.4 The Controller is responsible for notifying the ICO and affected data subjects where required by the Data Protection Legislation, based on its own assessment of the circumstances.

---

## 9. DATA SUBJECT ERASURE

9.1 One Assess shall process erasure requests for Controller Personal Data within 30 days of a confirmed instruction from the Controller or an authenticated erasure request from a data subject via the platform's built-in erasure flow.

9.2 Upon completion of erasure, One Assess shall provide written confirmation to the Controller identifying the data erased and the date of erasure.

9.3 Erasure records (documenting that erasure took place, by whom, and when) shall be retained by One Assess as a legal compliance record for a period of 3 years following the erasure event.

---

## 10. TERM AND TERMINATION

10.1 This DPA is effective from the date the Controller accepts the Main Agreement and remains in force until the Main Agreement terminates or expires.

10.2 The obligations in Clause 4.3 (security) and Clause 9 (erasure) survive termination of this DPA for as long as One Assess holds any Controller Personal Data.

---

## 11. LIABILITY

11.1 Each Party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations and exclusions set out in the Main Agreement, except that neither Party limits its liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation, or any other matter that cannot lawfully be excluded.

11.2 Where both Parties are responsible for damage caused by processing in breach of the Data Protection Legislation, each Party shall be liable for its own share of responsibility, as determined by applicable law.

## SCHEDULE 2 — TECHNICAL AND ORGANISATIONAL SECURITY MEASURES

One Assess implements and maintains the following measures:

**Access control**
- Role-based access controls: coaches access only their own clients' data; org admins access only their organisation's data; platform administrators require explicit `dataAccessPermission` flags
- Firebase Authentication with email/password and Google OAuth; session tokens not stored in a manner accessible to the application layer
- Multi-factor authentication available for coach accounts **[REVIEW: confirm MFA is implemented or note as roadmap item]**

**Data in transit**
- All data transmitted over TLS 1.2 or higher
- Firebase Storage and Firestore connections encrypted in transit

**Data at rest**
- Firebase Firestore and Firebase Storage data encrypted at rest by Google Cloud Platform using AES-256
- Posture photographs stored in Firebase Storage with access restricted to authenticated users within the relevant organisation

**Application security**
- Firebase App Check enforced on all Cloud Functions in production to prevent unauthorised API access
- Firestore security rules enforce data minimisation: each query is scoped to the authenticated user's organisation and role
- Input validation on all API endpoints; no raw user input passed to database queries

**Deletion and erasure**
- Automated erasure Cloud Function performs cascading deletion across all Firestore subcollections and Firebase Storage objects upon confirmed erasure request
- Erasure records retained in an append-only audit log

**Incident response**
- Sentry error monitoring with alerting for application errors
- Slack-based operational alerts for anomalous activity, billing events, and capacity thresholds

**Organisational measures**
- Personnel with access to production data are subject to confidentiality obligations
- Third-party sub-processor agreements include data protection obligations
